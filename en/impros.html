SFI/ILS

solo bass guitar performance

SFI/ILS is a solo performance in a somewhat constant development. It's articulated through a Performance as Research methodology, interested in the experimentation with physicality in music performances - specially in my own e-bass improvisations.

The performance focuses on explorations of how physicality in its relations and articulations with sound, corporality and temporality can shape our perceptions of presence and, with that, what an improvised e-bass performance is. Trying to separate itself from a type of music performance that is supposed to be articulated only in the realm of a crystalized and imaterial "music perception" - that tends to create a dissociation with the performance itself - it strives constantly to presentify itself, using repetition in multiple layers (structure, form, physicality, ...) to achieve this goal.

In 2024, it was presented three times in different occasions, formats and with different focuses. Here, you can watch its first public performance register.

PING (2020)

nao≠igual interactive duo

PING is inspired by PONG, the classic ATARI game, reimagined as an audiovisual system that interacts with musical improvisation, where each side of this ping-pong is controlled by a musician who, instead of using a joystick (or paddle), plays their instrument.

As in the original, PING is a competition between two sides, but here, these sides can reverse, multiply, expand, contract, or even bounce across the screen... and all this movement is controlled by the players through melodies, intensities, and rhythms.

With the players 500 km apart, we present an "online version" of the game, with real-time audio exchange, processing, and analysis performed using patches in Pd that communicate with a sketch built in Processing3.
